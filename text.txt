*Title:* The Myth of Happiness

*Scripture:* _Ecclesiastes 2:11_

*Reflection on Scripture:*
Solomon had pleasure, wealth, achievement, and influence, yet he called it vanity. This exposes the lie that happiness is the highest goal. Anything outside God may excite the soul temporarily but cannot anchor it eternally.

*Ancient Biblical Precedent:*
Solomon pursued pleasure, wisdom, building projects, wealth, and greatness, but still discovered emptiness without God.

*Testimony:*
Many people receive what they prayed for—marriage, money, promotion, recognition—yet still feel empty. That emptiness becomes God’s invitation to deeper truth.

*Scriptures:*
⦁ _Ecclesiastes 2:11_
⦁ _Psalm 16:11_
⦁ _Matthew 6:33_
⦁ _John 6:35_
⦁ _Colossians 3:2_

*Strategic Prayers:*
⦁ Lord, expose every false altar I trusted.
⦁ Break the illusion that happiness is my final goal.
⦁ Deliver me from chasing what cannot satisfy.
⦁ Redirect my desires toward eternal fulfillment.
⦁ Teach me joy rooted in Your presence.

*Activation and Manifestation:*
Write down what you thought would satisfy you. Lay it before God and surrender it.

*Declarations:*
I declare that my soul will not be ruled by false happiness.
I receive joy from God, not from circumstances.
My satisfaction is found in Christ alone.

*Title:* Evangelism is Relationship

*Reflection on Scripture:*
The gospel often travels through trust. People open their hearts when they feel seen, valued, and loved.

*Ancient Biblical Precedent:*
Jesus built relationship with His disciples before sending them. He ate with sinners, touched the rejected, and spoke to the overlooked.

*Testimony:*
Many people came to Christ because someone loved them consistently before preaching to them.

*Scriptures:*
⦁ _John 13:35_
⦁ _Luke 19:5–10_
⦁ _Mark 2:15–17_
⦁ _1 Thessalonians 2:8_
⦁ _Proverbs 18:24_

*Strategic Prayers:*
⦁ Lord, make me relational, not transactional.
⦁ Teach me to love people beyond projects.
⦁ Help me build trust with sincerity.
⦁ Remove hidden agendas from my heart.
⦁ Let my relationships reveal Your kindness.

*Activation and Manifestation:*
Have one meaningful conversation without trying to correct or convince.

*Declarations:*
⦁ I love people sincerely.
⦁ My relationships will reveal Christ.
⦁ The gospel flows through genuine connection.

*Title:* Commissioned to the Broken

*Scripture:* _Matthew 5:14_

*Apostolic Charge*

You are not sent to impress people—you are sent to sit with them, love them, and reveal Christ in their sorrow. The world does not only need louder voices; it needs living witnesses of divine consolation.

*Reflection on Scripture:*
Jesus calls His people the light of the world. Light does not perform; it shines. Your life becomes light when Christ’s compassion, truth, and presence move through you.

*Ancient Biblical Precedent:*
Jesus sent His disciples to heal the sick, preach the kingdom, cast out darkness, and carry peace into homes and cities.

*Testimony:*
Many broken people encountered God because someone became a visible expression of His love.

*Scriptures:*
⦁ _Matthew 5:14_
⦁ _Matthew 10:7–8_
⦁ _Isaiah 60:1_
⦁ _John 20:21_
⦁ _2 Corinthians 5:20_

*Strategic Prayers:*
⦁ Lord, make me a vessel of Your presence in a hurting world.
⦁ Send me to the broken with humility and compassion.
⦁ Let my life shine with Your light.
⦁ Remove pride, performance, and fear from my witness.
⦁ Use me to reveal Christ in sorrowful places.

*Activation and Manifestation:*
Ask God: “Who are You sending me to comfort, sit with, and love?”

*Prophetic Declaration*

“I am a carrier of divine consolation. Through me, Christ meets the broken.”

*Declarations:*
⦁ I am light in dark places.
⦁ I am commissioned to the broken.
⦁ Christ will be revealed through my compassion.
⦁ I carry divine consolation into a hurting world
